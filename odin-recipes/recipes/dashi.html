Dashi

Description

Dashi is simple Japanese soup broth, which acts as the base for many popular dishes such as miso soup, ramen and shabu shabu. The dashi in this recipe is called Awase Dashi, and uses a combination of dried seaweed and bonito flakes. Awase means 'to combine' in Japanese

Thank you to Nami from JustOneCookBook for her recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 Piece Konbu (10g dried kelp)
  • 1 Cup Katsuobushi (10g dried bonito flakes)
  • 4 Cups Water

Steps

  1. Make several slits to the konbu to help it release more flavour
  2. Add water and konbu to a medium saucepan
  3. Turn heat to medium low and slowly bring to boiling
  4. At the same time, clean the dashi by skimming the debris and foam from the surface
  5. Just before the dashi begins to boil, remove the konbu (if you leave it in, the dashi will become slimy and bitter)
  6. Add one cup fo bonito flakes and bring to boil again
  7. Once the dashi is boiling, reduce the heat and simmer for 30 seconds, then turn off the heat
  8. Let the dashi rest for 10 minutes or until the bonito flakes sink to the bottom
  9. Strain the dashi through a fine-mesh sieve
  10. The dashi is ready!
